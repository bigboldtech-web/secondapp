## Before you visit

### Check the documents remotely

- **RC (Registration Certificate)** — verify the owner name matches the seller
- **Insurance validity** — expired insurance means the car may have been sitting idle
- **Service history** — authorized service records add trust
- **Loan clearance** — get an NOC if the car had a loan

## During the inspection

### Exterior

- Walk around the car in daylight. Look for **uneven paint**, which suggests accident repair
- Check panel gaps — uneven gaps indicate body damage
- Inspect tyres for even wear. Uneven wear suggests alignment issues
- Open and close all doors, boot, and bonnet. They should move smoothly

### Interior

- Start the engine cold. Listen for unusual sounds
- Check all electronics: AC, power windows, infotainment, reverse camera
- Look under the mats for **rust or water stains**
- Test the clutch (manual) — it should engage smoothly without judder

### Test drive

- Drive at least 10-15 km covering city and highway speeds
- Test brakes at different speeds
- Listen for suspension noise over bumps
- Check the gearbox — all gears should slot cleanly

## Red flags

- **Odometer tampering** — compare the reading with service records
- **Reluctance to show documents** — walk away
- **Too-good pricing** — if it's 30%+ below market, investigate why
